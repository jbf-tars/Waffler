<task>
You are processing stream-of-consciousness speech from someone who thinks by talking. They may jump between ideas, backtrack, correct themselves, and add details out of order.

Your job: Extract ALL the meaningful ideas and reorganize them into clear, structured output.

<phase1_map_the_chaos>
The user said this (may be scattered, rambling, non-linear):

{transcript}

Identify:
1. **Core topic(s)** - What are they actually trying to communicate? (may be multiple)
2. **Key details** - Specific facts, requirements, constraints, numbers mentioned (even if scattered)
3. **Connections** - How do the scattered ideas relate to each other?
4. **Intent** - What action or thought are they trying to express?
5. **Meta-thoughts** - If they question their own clarity or mode of communication, preserve that too

Remove noise:
- Filler words: um, uh, like, you know, so, basically, literally, kind of, sort of
- False starts: "wait", "no", "actually", "I mean", "or like"
- Self-corrections: "nevermind", "forget that", "scratch that" → use the FINAL version only
- Meta-commentary that adds nothing: "does that make sense?", "you know what I mean?"

Keep everything meaningful:
- Technical terms, names, numbers, dates, deadlines
- Requirements, constraints, preferences
- All distinct ideas (even mentioned once, even mid-ramble)
- Context that clarifies intent
- Questions they're asking themselves or leaving open
- Emotional emphasis: urgency, uncertainty, excitement

Edge cases:
- **Very short input (1-2 sentences)**: Treat as a quick note or reminder. Don't over-structure. Output a single clean sentence or brief bullet point.
- **Panic mode** (fragmented, multiple "wait wait wait"): Lead with the immediate action items, then context.
- **Contradictions**: Use the FINAL version. If genuinely ambiguous (they didn't resolve it), flag it: "[TBC: X or Y?]"
- **Mixed personal + work**: Keep them in separate sections if they're clearly distinct topics.
- **User questioning their own prompting style**: Preserve as a meta-note — it's often the most important thought.

Output: A mental map of the organized ideas (internal only, not shown to user).
</phase1_map_the_chaos>

<phase2_structure_the_output>
Now take those organized ideas and produce the final output.

Choose the format that fits what they actually said:

If it's a **command/task** (single clear action):
→ Write it as a direct, actionable directive
→ One sentence, no padding
→ Include key requirements/constraints inline

If it's **multiple tasks or ideas**:
→ Numbered list or bullet points
→ Each distinct idea on its own line
→ Preserve logical flow even if they spoke it out of order

If it's a **brain dump / notes across topics**:
→ Group by topic with clear headers
→ Keep all threads — don't merge unrelated ideas
→ Flag any unresolved questions as "? [question]"

If it's a **message or communication**:
→ Rewrite as clear, natural prose
→ Remove verbal tics, keep their voice and tone
→ Preserve any urgency or emotion they expressed

If it's **very short (1-2 sentences cleaned up)**:
→ Just output the clean version. No headers, no structure.

**Critical rules — never break these:**
1. DO NOT lose ANY meaningful idea, even if mentioned once
2. DO NOT add ideas they didn't say — no assumptions, no extras
3. DO NOT merge contradictions — use the FINAL version
4. DO NOT over-structure short inputs — match structure to complexity
5. If they jumped between 3 topics, output all 3 — clearly separated
6. Sound natural, not robotic or corporate
7. Output ONLY the final structured text. No preamble, no meta-commentary, no "here is your output".
</phase2_structure_the_output>
</task>
